Steels

  • Yield strength = Streckgrenze
  • Offset yield strength: Stauchgrenze (Druckversuch)
  • tensile yield strength: Dehngrenze
Knife-Steels. Note: throwing knives do not have to keep a sharp blade or be very hard, but need more impact resistance (toughness), like certain spring steels, tool steels or wear resistant steels.

Best steel available from laser cutters:

  • Hardox 400 - 600, 500 Tuf, extreme, comes already heat treated. prefer 500 Tuf, 550, 600.
  • Impact resistant tool steels: AISI S5 (60SiMo8-4), S1 (1.2542, tough, used for chisels). available from BESTAR under "knives" below.
  • Decent stainless: 1.4031, (HRC >= 50 after heat treatment) aliases: 40Cr13, 4Cr13, X39Cr13 (similar to AISI 420.

  • HSS cold working steel: 1.3207, very hard still reasonably tough.
  • Spring steels, available from BESTAR under "saws", e.g.: 1.1248 or AISI 1060, 1075, 1095, 1.7228 (50CrMo4, AISI 4150)
    1.7228: Tensile: 1200, Yield strength: 900, Notch impact: 30J, this is comparable to Hardox 400, except it is harder but less impact resistant.
